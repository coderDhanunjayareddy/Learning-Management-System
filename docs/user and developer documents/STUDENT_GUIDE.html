1. Getting Started

Creating Your Account & First Login

Step 1: Access the Login Page
  1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
  2. Go to your institution's SPECTROPY portal URL
  3. You should see the login screen
[SCREENSHOT 1: Login Page]
Show: Email field, Password field, Login button, Forgot password link
Step 2: Enter Your Credentials
  1. Enter the email address your school provided
  2. Enter your password (provided by your school)
  3. Click the "LOGIN" button
💡 Tip: Passwords are case-sensitive! If "abc123" is your password, "ABC123" won't work. Make sure caps lock is OFF.
⚠️ Important: If you see "Invalid email or password" error:
  • Check that you typed your email correctly
  • Check that caps lock is OFF
  • Verify you're using the email your school gave you
  • Contact your teacher or administrator if you still can't login

First Time Setup

On your first login, you may be asked to:
  • Confirm your email address
  • Update your password to something personal
  • Complete your user profile

4. Finding & Taking Exams

Accessing the Exam Section

Step 1: Navigate to Exams
  1. Click "Exams" in the left sidebar
  2. You'll see two tabs: "Available" and "My Results"
  3. The "Available" tab shows exams you can take
[SCREENSHOT 5: Exams Page - Available Tab]
Show: List of available exams with details, Start Exam buttons, exam status

Understanding Exam Details

Before starting an exam, check these details:
  • Exam Title - Name of the exam
  • Description - What topics are covered
  • Duration - How long you have (in minutes)
  • Total Questions - Number of questions you'll answer
  • Total Marks - Maximum possible score
  • Passing Score - Minimum needed to pass
  • Attempts Allowed - How many times you can take it
  • Attempts Used - How many you've already used
  • Status - Open, Closed, or Upcoming

Starting an Exam

Step 2: Click "Start Exam"
  1. Find the exam you want to take in the available list
  2. Click the "START EXAM" button
  3. A confirmation dialog will appear
  4. Review the details one more time
  5. Click "BEGIN" to start the exam

How to Answer Different Question Types

Multiple Choice - Single Answer (Choose ONE)

[SCREENSHOT 8: MCQ Single Answer Question]
Show: Question with radio button options, one selected
How to Answer:
  1. Read the question carefully
  2. Click on the radio button (○) next to your chosen option
  3. The button will fill in (●) to show it's selected
  4. You can click another option to change your answer
  5. Click "Next" to proceed to the next question
Example:
What is 2 + 2?
○ 3
● 4 ← You selected this
○ 5
○ 6

Multiple Choice - Multiple Answers (Choose ALL that apply)

[SCREENSHOT 9: MCQ Multiple Answer Question]
Show: Question with checkbox options, multiple checked
How to Answer:
  1. Read the question carefully - it will say "select ALL that apply"
  2. Click on each checkbox (☐) for correct options
  3. Checkboxes will show checkmarks (☑) when selected
  4. You can click multiple checkboxes
  5. Click again to deselect if you change your mind
  6. Click "Next" to proceed
⚠️ Important for Multiple Answer Questions: You MUST select ALL correct answers. If you miss even one or select an incorrect one, you get zero points. The system is all-or-nothing!
Example:
Which of these are prime numbers?
☑ 7 ← Correct
☐ 8
☑ 11 ← Correct
☐ 15
☑ 19 ← Correct

True/False

[SCREENSHOT 10: True/False Question]
Show: Question with True and False buttons
How to Answer:
  1. Read the statement carefully
  2. Determine if it's true or false
  3. Click either the "TRUE" or "FALSE" button
  4. The button will highlight showing your selection
  5. Click the other button to change your answer
  6. Click "Next" to proceed
Example:
The Earth orbits the Sun.
☑ TRUE ← You selected this
☐ FALSE

Numerical Answer (Enter a number)

[SCREENSHOT 11: Numerical Answer Question]
Show: Question with text input field for number entry
How to Answer:
  1. Read the question and any special instructions
  2. Click in the answer field (text box)
  3. Type your numerical answer (can include decimals)
  4. The system accepts: integers, decimals, and negative numbers
  5. You can clear and retype to change your answer
  6. Click "Next" to proceed
💡 Tip: The system may accept a range of answers. For example, if the correct answer is 9.8 with a tolerance of ±0.1, you can answer anything from 9.7 to 9.9 and get full marks.
Example:
If radius = 5cm, what is the circumference? (use π ≈ 3.14)


Your answer might be: 31.4 or 31.40 (both accepted)

During the Exam - Important Actions

Navigating Between Questions

You have multiple ways to navigate:
  • Click "Next" button - Go to the next question in order
  • Click "Previous" button - Go back to previous question
  • Click question number in left panel to jump to any question
  • You can answer questions in ANY order - not required to go sequentially

Mark for Review

What is "Mark for Review"?

If you're unsure about an answer, click "Mark for Review" button. This:

  • Flags the question with a blue marker in the navigator
  • Still submits your current answer when you finish
  • Helps you find questions to double-check before submitting
  • Marked questions count as answered and get graded

Auto-Save Feature

Your answers are automatically saved every 30 seconds!
  • Look for "Saving..." indicator on the right panel
  • Your answers are saved to the server automatically
  • If internet drops, your answers up to last save are safe
  • When you reconnect, you continue from where you left off
💡 Pro Tip: You can also click "Save" button manually if you want immediate confirmation your answer is saved, even though auto-save is always working.

Submitting Your Exam

Step 3: When Ready to Submit
  1. Answer as many questions as you can
  2. Review your answers by looking at the question navigator (green = answered)
  3. Click the "SUBMIT EXAM" button (bottom right of exam)
[SCREENSHOT 12: Submit Exam Dialog]
Show: Confirmation dialog with question counts (answered/unanswered), warning, Submit/Cancel buttons
Step 4: Confirmation Dialog
  1. A dialog will appear showing:
    • Number of questions answered
    • Number of unanswered questions
  2. Review this carefully - are you sure you want to submit?
  3. If you want to go back and answer more, click "Cancel"
  4. If you're ready, click "Confirm Submission"
⚠️ Important: Once you submit, you CANNOT go back and change your answers. Make sure you're happy with your responses before clicking "Confirm Submission"!

What Happens After Submission

  • Your exam is automatically graded
  • You'll see a "Exam Submitted Successfully" message
  • You'll be shown your initial score
  • You'll be redirected to the results page
  • Results also appear in "My Results" section for later review

5. Exam Tips & Best Practices

Before the Exam

Preparation Tips:
  • ✓ Test your internet connection - make sure it's stable
  • ✓ Use a computer if possible (better than mobile)
  • ✓ Close other browser tabs and applications
  • ✓ Disable notifications from email, chat, etc.
  • ✓ Use the same browser you used for testing (Chrome recommended)
  • ✓ Have a bottle of water nearby
  • ✓ Use the bathroom before starting
  • ✓ Make sure you have the full duration available (no interruptions)

During the Exam

Exam-Taking Strategy:
  • ✓ Read each question TWICE before answering
  • ✓ Start with easier questions to build confidence
  • ✓ Skip difficult questions and come back to them
  • ✓ Manage your time - check the timer periodically
  • ✓ Mark uncertain answers for review with the flag button
  • ✓ Don't leave questions blank if possible
  • ✓ Double-check multiple answer questions (must select ALL correct)
  • ✓ For numerical questions, double-check your math

Time Management During Exam

Example Time Allocation (for 60-minute exam with 30 questions):
  • 0-2 minutes: Read through all questions (don't answer yet)
  • 2-45 minutes: Answer all questions you know (spend ~1.5 min per question)
  • 45-58 minutes: Review marked questions and uncertain answers
  • 58-60 minutes: Final review before submitting

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Don't:
  • ✗ Rush through questions - read carefully
  • ✗ Leave questions blank (unless instructed)
  • ✗ Overthink your answer once selected
  • ✗ Change answers multiple times unless you're sure
  • ✗ Forget to submit (exam won't submit automatically)
  • ✗ Rely only on your browser's back button
  • ✗ Switch browsers mid-exam
  • ✗ Assume the system will let you go over time

7. Troubleshooting

Login Problems

Problem: Can't login - "Invalid email or password"

Solutions:
  1. Check caps lock - password is case sensitive
  2. Verify correct email address (check for typos)
  3. Make sure you're entering the right password
  4. Try copying/pasting your email from your school's email
  5. If completely forgotten, contact your teacher for a password reset

Exam-Related Issues

Problem: Exam not available / "Exam not found"

Possible causes & solutions:
  1. Exam hasn't started yet - Check start date/time. Is it in the future?
  2. Exam has closed - Check end date. Is it in the past?
  3. You exceeded max attempts - Have you tried the maximum times allowed?
  4. You're not enrolled - Contact teacher to verify enrollment
  5. Browser issue - Try refreshing page or different browser
Action: Contact your teacher with the exam name and date
Problem: Internet dropped during exam

What happens:
  • Don't panic! Your answers are saved every 30 seconds
  • The exam stays "in progress"
  • When you reconnect, you can resume from where you left off
What to do:
  1. Reconnect to internet immediately
  2. Refresh the page
  3. You should be back in the exam at the question you were on
  4. Continue answering questions
  5. Submit when done as normal
Problem: Answer won't save or shows error message

Solutions:
  1. Check your internet connection (run speedtest.net)
  2. Look for "Saving..." indicator - wait for it to complete
  3. Manually click "Save" button
  4. Try refreshing the page
  5. Try closing exam and reopening (your answer will be there)
  6. If error persists, screenshot the error and email to teacher
Problem: Timer seems to be running fast or slow

Explanation:
  • Timer is server-based, not browser-based (accurate)
  • Your computer clock might be slightly off
  • Browser might have cached old time data
Solution:
  1. Refresh the page to sync timer with server
  2. Check your system clock is set correctly
  3. If still wrong after refresh, contact teacher/admin

Browser & Technical Issues

Problem: Page loads slowly or won't load

Try these steps in order:
  1. Hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac)
  2. Clear browser cache: Settings → Clear browsing data
  3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
  4. Try on a different device (if available)
  5. Close other apps/tabs that use internet
  6. Wait a few minutes and try again
💡 Browser Recommendations:
  • ✓ Chrome - Recommended (most reliable)
  • ✓ Firefox - Good
  • ✓ Edge - Good
  • ✓ Safari - Good (Mac)
  • ✗ Avoid: Internet Explorer (very old)

Getting Help

When to Contact Support:
  1. Contact your teacher if:
    • You can't access an exam
    • Exam doesn't show up but should be available
    • You think there's an error in a question
    • You need feedback on results
  2. Contact your school admin if:
    • You can't login
    • You forgot your password
    • Technical issue persists after troubleshooting
    • Something seems broken in the system
When contacting support, include:
  • Your full name and email
  • What you were trying to do
  • What error message you saw (screenshot helpful)
  • What browser you're using
  • What device (computer, tablet, phone)
  • What time it happened

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I take an exam multiple times?
A: Yes, if your teacher allows multiple attempts. Check the exam details for "Attempts Allowed".

Q: Will I see my score immediately?
A: Yes, usually within seconds. The system auto-grades all questions.

Q: Can I see the correct answers?
A: Yes, but only if your teacher enables "Show Answers After Submission". Check the results page.

Q: What if I submitted but didn't mean to?
A: Unfortunately, submitted exams can't be reopened. Be sure before clicking confirm!

Q: Can I download my results?
A: Use the Print function (Ctrl+P) and choose "Save as PDF" to create a copy.
