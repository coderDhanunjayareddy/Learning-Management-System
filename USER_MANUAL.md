## INTRODUCTION

Welcome to **SPECTROPY**, your comprehensive Learning Management System (LMS)! This manual will guide you through all the features and functionalities of our platform, whether you're a student taking exams, a teacher creating educational content, or an administrator managing the system.

### What is SPECTROPY?

SPECTROPY is a modern, intuitive learning platform designed to:
- ✓ Create and manage a comprehensive question bank
- ✓ Build and administer online exams
- ✓ Track student progress and results

## GETTING STARTED

### System Access

To access SPECTROPY, open your web browser and navigate to your institution's LMS portal. The system is optimized for:
- Desktop browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Tablet devices (iPad, Android tablets)
- Responsive design for mobile viewing

### Logging In

1. **Enter Your Credentials**
   - Navigate to the login page
   - Enter your email address in the "Email" field
   - Enter your password in the "Password" field

3. **First Login**

## FOR STUDENTS

### Student Dashboard Overview

Your student dashboard gives you a quick overview of:
- **Enrolled Courses**: List of all courses you're enrolled in
- **Available Exams**: Exams you're eligible to take
- **Exam Results**: History of exams you've completed

### Viewing Enrolled Courses

**To Access Your Courses:**
1. Click **"Courses"** in the sidebar navigation
2. You'll see all courses you're currently enrolled in
3. Each course card shows:
   - Course title and description
   - Course status (Active, Completed, Draft)
   - Number of lessons/content items
   - Your progress percentage

**To View Course Content:**
1. Click on a course card
2. Browse the course content:
   - Documents (PDF, Word, etc.)
   - Links to external resources
   - Video materials (if available)
   - Folders organizing content
3. Download or view content as needed
4. Mark items as complete using the checkbox (if available)

### Taking an Exam

#### Finding Available Exams

**Step 1: Navigate to Exams**
1. Click **"Exams"** in the sidebar
2. You'll see two sections:
   - **Available Exams**: Exams you can take now
   - **My Results**: Exams you've already completed

**Step 2: Check Exam Details**
Before starting an exam, review:
- **Exam Title**: The name of the exam
- **Duration**: How long you have (in minutes)
- **Description**: What the exam covers
- **Attempts**: How many times you can attempt it
- **Status**: Whether it's open or closed

#### Starting an Exam

**Step 1: Click "Start Exam"**
1. Find the exam you want to take
2. Click the **"Start Exam"** button
3. A confirmation dialog will appear
4. Click **"Begin"** to start

**Step 2: Exam Interface Overview**

When the exam loads, you'll see:

**Left Panel - Question Navigator**
- List of all questions in the exam
- Navigation buttons (Previous, Next)
- Question status indicators:
  - 🟢 Green: Question answered
  - 🔵 Blue: Marked for review
  - ⚪ White: Not answered

**Center Panel - Question Display**
- The current question text
- Question type (Multiple Choice, True/False, Numerical, etc.)
- Answer options or input field
- Buttons: Mark for Review, Clear Answer, Save

**Right Panel - Exam Timer & Status**
- Countdown timer showing remaining time
- Section progress
- Total marks
- Auto-save indicator (saves every 30 seconds)

#### How to Answer Questions

**For Multiple Choice (Single Answer)**
1. Read the question carefully
2. Click on one of the option buttons (A, B, C, D, etc.)
3. The selected option will highlight
4. Click **"Next"** to move to the next question OR click another option to change your answer

**For Multiple Choice (Multiple Answers)**
1. Read the question carefully
2. Click on each correct option (multiple selections allowed)
3. Selected options will show checkmarks
4. You can deselect by clicking again
5. Click **"Next"** to proceed

**For True/False Questions**
1. Click the **"True"** or **"False"** button
2. Your selection will highlight
3. Click the other button to change your answer
4. Click **"Next"** to proceed

**For Numerical Questions**
1. Click in the answer field
2. Type your numerical answer
3. The system will accept integers and decimal values
4. Some questions may have a tolerance range (e.g., ±0.01)
5. Click **"Next"** to proceed

#### During the Exam

**Marking Questions for Review**
- Not sure about an answer? Click **"Mark for Review"** (flag icon)
- Your question will be marked blue in the navigator
- You can review it later
- Marked questions are still submitted with your answers

**Saving Your Work**
- The system auto-saves every 30 seconds (look for "Saving..." indicator)
- You can also manually click **"Save"** at any time
- If you lose internet connection, your recent answers are saved

**Navigating Questions**
- Use **"Previous"** and **"Next"** buttons to move between questions
- Click directly on question numbers in the left panel to jump to a question
- You can navigate in any order; you don't have to answer sequentially

**Exam Timer Warnings**
- ⏱️ 10 minutes remaining: Timer text turns orange
- ⏱️ 1 minute remaining: Timer text turns red
- ⏱️ 0 minutes: Exam auto-submits automatically

#### Submitting Your Exam

**When You're Ready to Submit:**
1. Review the question navigator to check your answers
2. Click the **"Submit Exam"** button (bottom right)
3. A confirmation dialog appears listing:
   - Answered questions count
   - Unanswered questions count
4. Click **"Confirm Submission"** to finalize

**After Submission:**
- Your exam is graded automatically
- You'll see an immediate confirmation
- Results will appear in your "My Results" section

### Viewing Exam Results

**Step 1: Access Your Results**
1. Go to **"Exams"** → **"My Results"**
2. Click on any completed exam

**Step 2: Review Your Performance**

Your result report shows:
- **Total Score**: Your final mark (e.g., 75/100)
- **Percentage**: Your score as a percentage
- **Section Breakdown** (if exam had multiple sections):
  - Score for each section
  - Percentage per section
- **Time Taken**: How long you spent on the exam
- **Status**: Passed/Failed (if pass criteria set)

**Step 3: Review Question-by-Question**

For each question, you'll see:
- The original question text
- Your answer (what you selected)
- The correct answer
- Whether you got it right ✓ or wrong ✗
- The solution explanation (if provided by instructor)
- Marks awarded

### Creating and Managing Questions

#### Accessing the Question Bank

**Step 1: Navigate to Questions**
1. Click **"Questions"** in the sidebar
2. You'll see:
   - **Search & Filter** panel (top)
   - **Question List** (center)
   - **Create Question** button (top-right)

**Step 2: Search Existing Questions**

Filter questions by:
- **Subject**: Select academic subject
- **Chapter**: Chapter within the subject
- **Topic**: Specific topic area
- **Difficulty**: Easy, Medium, Hard
- **Question Type**: MCQ, True/False, Numerical, etc.
- **Status**: Draft, Approved, Rejected, Archived
- **Keyword**: Full-text search across question text

#### Creating a New Question

**Step 1: Click "Create Question"**
1. Navigate to the Questions section
2. Click the **"+ Create Question"** button
3. A question creation form opens

**Step 2: Enter Question Details**

**Basic Information Section:**
- **Question Type**: Select from dropdown
  - Multiple Choice (Single Answer)
  - Multiple Choice (Multiple Answers)
  - True/False
  - Numerical
- **Subject**: Choose from dropdown (e.g., Mathematics, Science)
- **Chapter**: Select relevant chapter
- **Topic**: Select topic within chapter
- **Difficulty Level**: Easy / Medium / Hard

**Question Content Section:**
- **Question Text**:
  - Click in the rich text editor
  - Type or paste your question
  - Use formatting: Bold, Italic, Underline, Lists, etc.
  - Insert LaTeX for mathematical formulas: `$formula$`
  - Insert images: Click image icon, upload file

  *Example Question:*
  "What is the value of $\sqrt{16}$ + 3?"

**Marking Scheme:**
- **Positive Marks**: Points awarded for correct answer (e.g., 1, 2, 4)
- **Negative Marks**: Points deducted for wrong answer (e.g., 0, 0.25, 0.5)
- Leave at 0 if no negative marking desired

**Step 3: Add Answer Options (Based on Question Type)**

**For Multiple Choice (Single Answer):**
1. Under "Options", you'll see fields for A, B, C, D, etc.
2. For each option:
   - Type the option text
   - Use rich text formatting if needed
   - Click the radio button to mark as correct answer
3. Example:
   - [ ] A) 15
   - [ ] B) 19  ← Select this as correct
   - [ ] C) 21
   - [ ] D) 25

**For Multiple Choice (Multiple Answers):**
1. For each option:
   - Type the option text
   - Check the checkbox if it's a correct answer
   - Multiple options can be marked correct
2. Example:
   - ☑ A) Paris
   - ☑ B) London  ← Multiple correct answers
   - ☐ C) Berlin
   - ☐ D) Madrid

**For True/False:**
1. Select **"True"** or **"False"** as the correct answer
2. Only one answer possible

**For Numerical:**
1. Enter the **"Expected Value"** (correct answer)
2. Enter **"Tolerance"** (acceptable range)
   - Example: If value is 9.8 with tolerance 0.1, answers from 9.7 to 9.9 are correct
   - Leave at 0 for exact match only

**Step 4: Add Optional Information**

**Solution/Explanation:**
- Click "Add Solution"
- Provide detailed explanation of correct answer
- Use rich text formatting
- Include worked examples if helpful
- This appears to students after they complete the exam

**Tags/Exam Tags:**
- Add relevant tags for organization (e.g., "Chapter5", "Physics", "Hard")
- Useful for searching and filtering later

**Step 5: Save the Question**

1. Click **"Save as Draft"**:
   - Question is saved but not yet ready for exams
   - Only visible to you
   - You can edit anytime

2. Click **"Submit for Approval"** (Alternative):
   - Question is submitted to admin for review
   - Status becomes "Pending Approval"
   - Admin checks correctness, clarity, and formatting
   - Once approved, you can use in exams
   - If rejected, admin provides feedback for revision

#### Editing an Existing Question

**To Edit a Question:**
1. Find the question in your question list
2. Click the **"Edit"** button (pencil icon)
3. Modify any fields
4. Click **"Update"** to save changes

**Important Notes:**
- Draft questions can be freely edited
- Approved questions can still be edited (edits re-enter approval flow)
- Once used in an exam attempt, questions cannot be deleted

#### Managing Question Status

**Question Status Workflow:**

1. **Draft**
   - Created by teacher
   - Only visible to creator
   - Can be edited or deleted freely
   - Cannot be used in exams

2. **Pending Approval**
   - Submitted by teacher
   - Awaiting admin review
   - Cannot be edited while pending
   - Cannot be used in exams yet

3. **Approved** ✓
   - Reviewed and approved by admin
   - Can now be added to exams
   - Can still be edited (goes back to pending)

4. **Rejected** ✗
   - Admin found issues
   - Admin feedback provided
   - Can be edited and resubmitted
   - Cannot be used in exams until approved

5. **Archived**
   - No longer in active use
   - Soft-deleted (not permanently removed)
   - Won't appear in searches by default

#### Accessing the Exam Manager

**Step 1: Navigate to Exams**
1. Click **"Exams"** in the sidebar
2. You'll see your exams organized by status:
   - **Draft**: In progress, not yet ready
   - **Published**: Ready for students to take
   - **Archived**: Old exams no longer in use

**Step 2: Search and Filter**
- Filter by:
  - Date created
  - Status (Draft, Published, Archived)

#### Creating a New Exam

**Step 1: Click "Create Exam"**
1. Click the **"+ Create Exam"** button
2. The exam creation form opens

**Step 2: Enter Exam Details**

**Basic Information:**
- **Exam Title**: Name of the exam (e.g., "Biology Midterm - Chapter 5-7")
- **Description**: What the exam covers, any special instructions
- **Duration**: Time allowed in minutes (e.g., 60, 90, 120)
- **Total Marks**: Maximum marks for the exam (e.g., 100)

**Exam Settings:**
- **Passing Score**: Minimum score needed to pass (e.g., 40% = 40 marks)
- **Max Attempts**: How many times students can attempt (1, 2, 3, or unlimited)
- **Shuffle Questions**: Enable to randomize question order for each student
- **Show Answers**: Enable to show answers after submission
- **Question Shuffling**: Enable to randomize option order within MCQ questions

**Availability Window:**
- **Start Date/Time**: When exam becomes available
- **End Date/Time**: When exam closes (after this, students can't start new attempts)
- **Buffer Time**: Additional time before exam closes for last submissions

**Step 3: Add Exam Sections (Optional)**

Many exams are organized into sections:

1. Click **"Add Section"**
2. Enter section details:
   - **Section Name**: e.g., "Physics Questions", "Problem Solving"
   - **Section Time Limit**: Separate timer for this section (optional)
   - **Marks**: Points allocated to this section
3. Repeat for each section needed

**Step 4: Add Questions to Exam**

**Method 1: Add Individual Questions**
1. Click **"Add Questions"** button
2. A question bank dialog opens
3. Browse or search questions:
   - Use filters (Subject, Chapter, Difficulty, Type)
   - Click **"Select"** on each question to add
4. Questions are added to current section
5. Assign marks per question (if different from default)

**Method 2: Add All Questions from Topic**
1. Click **"Add Topic"**
2. Select Subject → Chapter → Topic
3. All approved questions from that topic are added
4. Assign marks as needed

**Step 5: Review Exam Structure**

Before publishing, review:
- ✓ All sections and questions are present
- ✓ Total marks add up correctly
- ✓ Difficulty mix is appropriate
- ✓ No duplicate questions
- ✓ All questions are approved
- ✓ Timing is appropriate for number of questions

**Step 6: Save and Publish**

**Save as Draft:**
1. Click **"Save as Draft"**
2. Exam is saved but not visible to students
3. You can continue editing

**Publish Exam:**
1. Click **"Publish"**
2. Exam becomes visible to enrolled students
3. Students can start attempting within the availability window
4. Once published, limited editing (cannot remove questions with attempts)

#### Managing Exams After Creation

**Editing an Exam:**
- Draft exams: Fully editable
- Published exams: Can update description, dates, shuffling settings

**Viewing Exam Results:**
1. Click on exam → **"View Results"**

## QUESTION BANK & EXAM ENGINE

### Understanding Question Types

SPECTROPY supports four main question types:

#### 1. Multiple Choice - Single Answer (MCQ)

**Description**: Select the ONE correct option from multiple choices.

**Example:**
```
What is the capital of France?
A) London
B) Berlin
C) Paris ← Correct
D) Madrid
```

**Grading**: All-or-nothing (full marks or zero)

**Use Case**: Testing factual knowledge, straightforward concepts

---

#### 2. Multiple Choice - Multiple Answers

**Description**: Select ALL correct options (more than one correct answer).

**Example:**
```
Which of the following are prime numbers?
A) 7 ✓
B) 12
C) 11 ✓
D) 15
E) 19 ✓
```

**Grading**:
- All-or-nothing (student must select ALL correct options, no extras)
- OR partial credit version (based on configuration)

**Use Case**: Testing comprehensive understanding, multiple valid points

**Challenge**: Students must identify ALL correct answers; partial selection gets zero

---

#### 3. True/False

**Description**: Determine if a statement is true or false.

**Example:**
```
"The Earth orbits the Sun."
[ ] True  [✓] False  ← Correct answer
```

**Grading**: All-or-nothing

**Use Case**: Quick assessment, yes/no decisions, myths vs. facts

---

#### 4. Numerical Answer

**Description**: Enter a numerical value (integer or decimal).

**Example:**
```
If radius = 5cm, what is the area of the circle? (use π ≈ 3.14)
Answer: 78.5 cm²
(System accepts 78.4 to 78.6 if tolerance = 0.1)
```

**Grading**:
- **Exact Match**: Answer must be exactly correct (tolerance = 0)
- **Range/Tolerance**: Answer within acceptable range
  - Example: ±0.01, ±1%, ±5°

**Use Case**: Math, physics, engineering problems; calculations

**Special Features**:
- Accepts decimals
- Can specify units (system doesn't validate units yet)
- Tolerance helps account for rounding differences

---

### Building Effective Exams

#### Best Practices

**1. Balanced Difficulty**
- Mix Easy, Medium, and Hard questions
- Recommended ratio: 20% Easy, 50% Medium, 30% Hard
- Avoids exams that are too easy or impossibly hard

**2. Clear Timing**
- Allocate 1-2 minutes per question minimum
- Example: 30 questions × 2 min = 60 minute exam
- Include buffer for difficult questions

**3. Proper Marking Scheme**
- Consistent marks per question OR
- More marks for harder questions
- Example: Easy = 1 mark, Medium = 2 marks, Hard = 3 marks

**4. Question Organization**
- Group related questions in sections
- Progress from easy to hard (or by topic)
- Clearly label each section

**5. Negative Marking Strategy**
- Use negative marks to discourage guessing
- Formula: Negative = 1/4 of positive marks
- Example: +4 marks (correct), -1 mark (wrong), 0 (unanswered)
- OR no negative marking for mixed difficulty

**6. Solution Quality**
- Always provide solutions
- Include worked examples for math
- Explain why other options are wrong
- Help students learn from mistakes

### Questions for Students

**Q: Can I take an exam multiple times?**
A: Yes, if the teacher allows multiple attempts. Check exam details for maximum attempts allowed.

**Q: What happens if my internet cuts during an exam?**
A: Don't worry! Your answers are saved every 30 seconds. When you reconnect, you'll continue from where you left off.

**Q: Can I go back and change my answer?**
A: Yes, you can navigate freely between questions and change any answer before submitting.

**Q: What happens if I run out of time?**
A: Your exam automatically submits when the timer reaches zero. Any unsaved answers up to last auto-save are included.

**Q: Can I see my answers after submitting?**
A: Yes, if the teacher enables answer viewing after submission. Results will show your answer vs. correct answer.

**Q: What does "Marked for Review" do?**
A: It flags the question with a blue marker so you can easily find it. The question is still submitted with your answer.

**Q: My exam says "Awaiting to Start" — when can I begin?**
A: The exam hasn't started yet. Check the exam start date/time. It becomes available at the scheduled time.

---

### Questions for Teachers

**Q: Can I edit a question after submitting for approval?**
A: No, questions submitted for approval are locked. If rejected, you can edit the rejected version.

**Q: How long does question approval take?**
A: Typically 24 hours. Your admin may process faster. Check with your administrator.

**Q: Can I reorder questions in an exam?**
A: Yes, in draft exams you can drag-and-drop to reorder. Saving the order is automatic.

**Q: Can I use questions from other teachers?**
A: Only if the question is approved and visible in your question bank. Ownership isn't shared.

**Q: What if a student points out an error in my question?**
A: Contact your admin. They can archive the question and you can create a corrected version.

**Q: Can I create private exams for a specific batch?**
A: Yes, by assigning exams to specific batches during creation. Non-assigned students won't see them.

**Q: How do I give negative marks?**
A: While creating the question, enter the negative mark value (e.g., -0.25). Exam auto-calculates the score.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### Login Issues

**Problem: "Invalid email or password"**
- Ensure caps lock is OFF
- Check email spelling (typos are common)
- Verify you're using the correct email
- Contact admin if you've forgotten password

**Problem: "Account locked" or "Too many login attempts"**
- Wait 15 minutes before trying again
- Security feature to prevent hacking
- Contact admin if you're still locked out

**Problem: Browser shows "Server not responding"**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page (F5)
- Try a different browser
- Contact admin if issue persists

---

#### Exam Taking Issues

**Problem: "Exam not available"**
- Check the exam start date/time
- Ensure you haven't exceeded max attempts
- Verify you're enrolled in the course
- Contact teacher or admin

**Problem: Timer seems wrong or runs out too fast**
- Refresh the page (timer syncs with server)
- Ensure system clock is accurate
- Contact admin if timer is truly incorrect
- Remember: Timer is server-based, not browser-based

**Problem: Answer not saving**
- Look for "Saving..." indicator (appears every 30 seconds)
- Try clicking "Save" manually
- Check internet connection
- Try refreshing exam page (you'll return to last saved point)

**Problem: "This question has no correct answer selected"**
- Appears when you forgot to select an answer
- Either answer the question or mark for review
- Cannot submit exam with this error

**Problem: Can't upload image/attachment to exam**
- File size too large (max ~10MB)
- Unsupported file type (check accepted formats)
- Try compressing image before uploading
- Contact admin for other issues

---

#### Question Creation Issues

**Problem: LaTeX formula not rendering**
- Use correct LaTeX syntax: `$...$` for inline, `$$...$$` for block
- Example: `$\sqrt{16}$` → √16
- Common mistake: Missing $ delimiters
- Test formulas before submitting

**Problem: "Cannot submit — solution required"**
- Add solution/explanation to the question
- Solution helps students understand
- Click "Add Solution" button
- Provide detailed explanation

**Problem: Admin rejected question but no feedback**
- Check email for rejection details
- Log in and view question — rejection reason shown
- Contact admin if reason unclear
- Ask for specific suggestions for revision

---

#### Performance Issues

**Problem: Page loads slowly**
- Clear browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Del)
- Try incognito/private mode
- Disable browser extensions
- Check internet speed (try speedtest.net)
- Use modern browser (Chrome preferred)

**Problem: Exam exam becomes unresponsive/freezes**
- Try refreshing the page (you'll resume from last save)
- Close and reopen the exam
- Try different browser
- Check system resources (RAM, CPU)
- Contact admin if issue persists

**Problem: Large file won't upload**
- Split file into smaller sections
- Compress if it's an image/video
- Try uploading at off-peak hours
- Contact admin for file size limits
